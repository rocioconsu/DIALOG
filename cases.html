Selected Case Studies

1 / Augmented Reality

IKEA Place

IKEA Place revolutionized the furniture industry by solving the 'uncertainty gap' in online shopping. Using high-definition Augmented Reality (AR) and 3D modeling, the system allows users to digitally 'place' true-to-scale furniture in their own living spaces. The interaction is seamless: the app scans the room's dimensions and lighting to ensure the virtual object looks realistic. This dialogue between the user's physical environment and the digital product builds a bridge of trust, drastically reducing product returns and enhancing the decision-making process through immersive visualization.

7.2 / Phygital Systems

Nike House of Innovation

Nike's flagship stores represent the pinnacle of 'Phygital' design—the merging of physical and digital retail. These interactive systems transform the store into a responsive environment that recognizes the individual user. By integrating RFID technology, mobile app connectivity, and giant interactive canvases, the system offers personalized product data and instant customization options as the user moves through the space. It is a prime example of how graphic design and technology collaborate to guide human behavior.

3 / Facial Mapping & Personalization

Sephora Virtual Artist

The Sephora Virtual Artist is a sophisticated interactive tool that leverages advanced 3D facial recognition to democratize professional beauty advice. The system tracks the user's facial features in real-time, allowing them to test thousands of shades of lipstick, eyeshadow, and false lashes without ever touching a physical product. By providing immediate visual feedback and personalized color matching, the system empowers users to explore styles they might otherwise avoid. This data-driven interaction not only increases user engagement but also provides the brand with invaluable insights into consumer preferences and trends.
